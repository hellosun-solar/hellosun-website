Roofing

Your roof is the foundation
of everything that goes on it.

Most solar companies are not roofing companies. We are. Deep roots in the roofing industry mean we treat every penetration, every flashing detail, and every mount point the way a roofer does — not the way a solar installer hopes to get away with.

What We Hear. What We Do.

The roof problems solar companies create — and how we avoid them.

Most solar installers aren't roofers. The difference shows in the details.

Knowing how to install racking is not the same as knowing proper flashing technique, penetration integration, or how different roofing materials require different approaches. Work that passes inspection can still fail over time when the details aren't right.

HelloSun

We've spent time and sweat equity in the roofing industry. We understand how different roofing systems behave at penetration points — asphalt, metal, low-slope membranes. That knowledge goes into every system we install, whether we put the roof on or not.

Two contractors means two sets of fingers to point at each other.

When your solar company and your roofer are different companies, nobody fully owns the interface between them. When something leaks, the solar company points at the roofer. The roofer points at the penetrations. You're in the middle making calls and waiting for answers.

HelloSun

When HelloSun installs both the roof and the solar system, they're covered together — contractually. We guarantee the combination we installed won't leak. One company. One guarantee. No gaps.

Fly-by-night installers use deck mounts because they're fast. Fast isn't the same as right.

Direct-to-deck mounts drill straight into the roof deck and rely on sealant alone to keep water out — no flashing, no integration with the roofing system. Sealant degrades. These mounting systems haven't been truly vetted over decades of real-world conditions: freeze-thaw cycles, UV exposure, thermal movement. The industry moved fast and skipped that part.

HelloSun

We only install mechanically flashed mounts. Every attachment point is fully integrated with the roofing system — just like a pipe vent or skylight — the way it's been done on proper roofs for decades. We won't take chances with products that haven't been battle-tested because they claim to save a couple man-hours per job. It's not a compromise we're willing to make.

A failing roof under solar panels is an expensive problem.

If your roof needs replacing after solar is installed, every panel has to come off first. Removing and reinstalling an array adds thousands to a re-roof that didn't have to cost that much.

HelloSun

We assess your roof's condition before any system goes up. If it needs attention, we address it as part of the project — one team, one mobilization, no surprises later.

Solar + Roof Together

The best time to re-roof is before solar goes up.

If your roof has 5–8 years of useful life left, putting solar on it now means paying to remove and reinstall the entire array when it needs replacing. Coordinating both as a single project saves money and protects your solar investment from day one.

  • Re-roof and solar installation coordinated as a single project
